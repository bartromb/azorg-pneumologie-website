About the department

Pneumology at AZORG

The Pneumology department examines and treats diseases of the lungs and respiratory tract. Common examples are asthma, allergies, chronic cough, chronic bronchitis and infections. Sleep apnea also belongs to the domain of the lung diseases service.

Pulmonary embolism, pulmonary fibrosis and lung cancer are other conditions treated by the Pneumology department. Different lung diseases are approached in a multidisciplinary manner in which specialists are involved in the patient consultation.

In addition, nurses, psychologists and physiotherapists are also called in for integrated care. The team guarantees top care, with active participation in scientific research and training of young pulmonologists.

What we treat

  • Asthma
  • Allergy
  • COPD
  • Pneumonia (pneumonia)
  • Collapsed lung (pneumothorax)
  • Pulmonary embolism
  • Lung cancer
  • Sarcoidosis

Lung conditions

Conditions we treat

More information at azorg.be/longziekten.

🌬️ Asthma

Chronic inflammatory disease of the respiratory tract with attacks of shortness of breath, wheezing and cough.

More info →

🌿 Allergy

Hypersensitivity reactions to allergens such as pollen, dust mites or animals. Diagnosis via skin tests, treatment via antihistamines or immunotherapy.

More info →

🫁 COPD

Chronic obstructive pulmonary disease due to long-term airway inflammation, mainly due to smoking. Treatment via bronchodilators, rehabilitation and smoking cessation.

More info →

🦠 Pneumonia (pneumonia)

Infection of the lung tissue by bacteria, viruses or fungi.

More info →

💨 Collapsed lung (pneumothorax)

Spontaneous or traumatic lung collapse with air entering the pleural space.

More info →

🩸 Pulmonary embolism

Blood clot in the pulmonary arteries, often caused by deep vein thrombosis.

More info →

🔬 Lung cancer

Malignant tumor of the lung tissue. Multidisciplinary approach via weekly MOC consultation.

More info →

🔵 Sarcoidosis

Granulomatous disease that can affect the lungs, glands and other organs.

More info →

😴 Sleep apnea (OSAS)

Repeated breathing stops during sleep due to upper airway obstruction.

More info →

🫧 Interstitial lung diseases

Scarring of the lung tissue (pulmonary fibrosis) with progressive loss of lung capacity.

More info →

🌿 Pleural disorders

Fluid accumulation or inflammation in the pleural space.

More info →

🦠 Flu & infections

Viral and bacterial respiratory infections including influenza and tuberculosis.

More info →
Diagnostics & procedures

Pulmonary examinations and procedures

The department has modern diagnostic and interventional techniques.

🌡️

Allergy skin test

Prick test for identification of specific allergens.

🔭

Bronchoscopy

Internal keyhole surgery of the airways via flexible endoscope.

🩻

CT scan of the lungs

High-resolution imaging for screening and diagnosis.

🔬

Ultrasound endoscopy (EBUS)

Endoscopic ultrasound for minimally invasive lymph node biopsies.

💊

Histamine threshold determination

Provocation test for suspected asthma.

📊

Pulmonary function measurement

Spirometry, body plethysmography and diffusion measurement.

🫁

V/Q scan (lung perfusion)

Nuclear medicine examination for pulmonary embolism.

💉

Pleural puncture

Ultrasound-guided puncture of the pleural space.

😴

Polysomnography

Complete sleep study for sleep apnea diagnosis.

🔵

Rigid bronchoscopy

Interventional procedure for stenting or tumor treatment.

🩺

Thoracoscopy

Minimally invasive inspection of the pleural space.

🏃

Exercise Test (CPET)

Cardiopulmonary exercise test on bicycle.
