About the department

Pneumology at AZORG

The Pneumology department examines and treats diseases of the lungs and respiratory tract. Common examples are asthma, allergies, chronic cough, chronic bronchitis and infections. Sleep apnea also belongs to the domain of the lung diseases service.

Pulmonary embolism, pulmonary fibrosis and lung cancer are other conditions treated by the Pneumology department. Different lung diseases are approached in a multidisciplinary manner in which specialists are involved in the patient consultation.

In addition, nurses, psychologists and physiotherapists are also called in for integrated care. The team guarantees top care, with active participation in scientific research and training of young pulmonologists.

What we treat

  • Asthma
  • Allergy
  • COPD
  • Pneumonia (pneumonia)
  • Collapsed lung (pneumothorax)
  • Pulmonary embolism
  • Lung cancer
  • Sarcoidosis

Lung conditions

Conditions we treat

More information at azorg.be/longziekten.

🌬️ Asthma

In asthma, your airways are chronically inflamed and extra sensitive. This can lead to episodes of breathlessness, wheezing and coughing. With the right medication and follow-up, asthma can be kept well under control.

Symptoms

Breathlessness (especially at night or during exercise), wheezing, tightness in the chest, coughing fits. Symptoms often occur in episodes and can be triggered by cold air, exercise, pollen or dust mites.

Diagnosis

Pulmonary function test (spirometry) with reversibility test, peak flow measurement at home, possibly histamine provocation test or allergy tests (skin prick test or blood test).

Treatment

Inhaled medication: bronchodilators for acute symptoms, anti-inflammatory drugs (corticosteroids) as maintenance treatment. For severe asthma: biological therapy. Additionally: avoiding allergens, establishing an asthma action plan.

When to see a doctor?

If you have a persistent cough or wheezing for more than 3 weeks, night-time symptoms that disturb your sleep, or if your inhaler is not helping enough.

🌿 Allergy

Your body overreacts to substances such as pollen, dust mites or animal hair. We identify the allergen through skin tests or blood tests. Treatment consists of medication or desensitisation (immunotherapy).

Symptoms

Sneezing, runny nose, itchy/watery eyes, skin rash, breathlessness. In severe allergy: throat swelling, breathing difficulties (anaphylaxis — call 112).

Diagnosis

Skin prick test with common allergens, blood test (specific IgE), possibly provocation test. Results usually available the same day.

Treatment

Avoiding the allergen, antihistamines, nasal spray with corticosteroids. If insufficient effect: immunotherapy (desensitisation) — a 3 to 5 year course that tackles the allergy at its source.

When to see a doctor?

If you have recurring or worsening symptoms, insufficient effect from over-the-counter medication, or if you want to know which allergen you are reacting to.

🫁 COPD

COPD is a chronic lung disease in which your airways narrow, usually due to years of smoking. You experience increasing breathlessness, coughing and mucus. Smoking cessation, medication and pulmonary rehabilitation significantly slow the deterioration.

Symptoms

Increasing breathlessness (first during exercise, later at rest), chronic cough with mucus, rapid fatigue, frequent respiratory infections. Symptoms often worsen in autumn and winter.

Diagnosis

Spirometry (lung function test): if the airways do not open sufficiently after medication, this indicates COPD. Additionally: CT scan, blood gas analysis, exercise test (CPET) to assess severity.

Treatment

Smoking cessation is the most important step. Additionally: inhaled medication (bronchodilators + anti-inflammatory drugs), pulmonary rehabilitation, oxygen therapy for severe COPD, vaccination against influenza and pneumococcus.

When to see a doctor?

If you experience increasing breathlessness, cough lasting more than 3 weeks, coughing up blood or mucus, or if you are a (former) smoker over 40.

🦠 Pneumonia (pneumonia)

Pneumonia is an infection of the lung tissue caused by bacteria, viruses or fungi. Typical symptoms are fever, coughing, breathlessness and fatigue. Most cases of pneumonia recover well with the right treatment.

Symptoms

Fever (sometimes with chills), cough with mucus (sometimes greenish or bloody), breathlessness, chest pain on deep breathing, fatigue and general malaise.

Diagnosis

Clinical examination (listening to the lungs), blood tests (inflammatory markers), chest X-ray, possibly CT scan or sputum culture to identify the pathogen.

Treatment

Antibiotics for bacterial pneumonia, rest and fluid intake. In severe cases: hospitalisation with intravenous antibiotics and oxygen. Viral pneumonias usually recover with supportive care.

When to see a doctor?

If you have a fever above 38.5°C with cough and breathlessness, chest pain, or if symptoms do not improve after a week.

💨 Collapsed lung (pneumothorax)

In a pneumothorax, air escapes from the lung into the chest cavity, causing the lung to (partially) collapse. This can occur spontaneously or after trauma. You feel sudden stabbing chest pain and breathlessness.

Symptoms

Sudden, stabbing chest pain (on one side), breathlessness that worsens rapidly, sometimes dry cough. Occurs spontaneously (especially in young, slim men) or after trauma.

Diagnosis

Chest X-ray or CT scan shows the collapsed lung. In an emergency, bedside ultrasound can provide a rapid diagnosis.

Treatment

Small pneumothorax: observation and rest. Larger pneumothorax: air drainage via needle or chest tube. For recurrent pneumothorax: surgical intervention (pleurodesis or thoracoscopy).

When to see a doctor?

If you experience sudden one-sided chest pain with breathing difficulties: go to the emergency department immediately.

🩸 Pulmonary embolism

A pulmonary embolism occurs when a blood clot blocks a blood vessel in the lungs, usually originating from a leg vein. Symptoms include sudden breathlessness, chest pain and sometimes coughing up blood. Rapid diagnosis and blood thinners are essential.

Symptoms

Sudden breathlessness, stabbing chest pain (worse on deep breathing), rapid heartbeat, sometimes coughing up blood, dizziness. Risk factors: recent surgery, prolonged immobilisation, contraceptive pill, history of thrombosis.

Diagnosis

D-dimer blood test (for low suspicion), CT pulmonary angiography (gold standard). Sometimes additionally: leg ultrasound to detect deep vein thrombosis.

Treatment

Blood thinners (anticoagulants) for at least 3 months. For massive embolism: thrombolysis or catheter intervention. Afterwards: follow-up and possibly long-term blood thinning.

When to see a doctor?

If you experience sudden breathlessness with chest pain: call 112. This is an emergency.

🔬 Lung cancer

Lung cancer is a malignant tumour in the lung tissue. Symptoms such as persistent coughing, coughing up blood or weight loss often only appear at a late stage. At AZORG, we discuss every patient weekly in a multidisciplinary meeting (MDT) for the best possible treatment.

Symptoms

Persistent cough (or change in an existing cough), coughing up blood, unexplained weight loss, persistent fatigue, chest pain, recurrent lung infections. In an early stage, there are often no symptoms.

Diagnosis

CT scan of the lungs, PET scan, bronchoscopy with biopsy, EBUS (endobronchial ultrasound) for lymph node examination. Every patient is discussed at the weekly multidisciplinary team meeting (MDT).

Treatment

Depending on the stage: surgery, chemotherapy, immunotherapy, radiation, or a combination. The treatment plan is tailored by the MDT team (pulmonologist, surgeon, oncologist, radiologist, pathologist).

When to see a doctor?

If you have a persistent cough for more than 3 weeks, cough up blood, experience unexplained weight loss, or if you are a (former) smoker and concerned.

🔵 Sarcoidosis

In sarcoidosis, small inflammatory nodules (granulomas) form in your lungs, lymph nodes or other organs. The cause is unknown. Many patients have few symptoms and recover spontaneously; others require medication.

Symptoms

Often few symptoms at discovery. Possible symptoms: dry cough, fatigue, breathlessness on exertion, joint pain, skin abnormalities, fever. The disease can also affect the eyes, liver and heart.

Diagnosis

Chest X-ray and CT scan (enlarged lymph nodes, lung abnormalities), blood tests (ACE level, calcium), pulmonary function test. Sometimes tissue biopsy via bronchoscopy for confirmation.

Treatment

In mild forms: watchful waiting with regular check-ups. In more severe forms: corticosteroids (prednisolone), sometimes supplemented with methotrexate or other immunosuppressive medication.

When to see a doctor?

If you experience unexplained fatigue combined with coughing or breathlessness, or skin abnormalities that do not resolve.

😴 Sleep apnea (OSAS)

In sleep apnoea, your breathing repeatedly stops during sleep because the throat closes. You sleep poorly, snore loudly and feel tired during the day. After diagnosis through a sleep study, treatment with a CPAP mask or lifestyle changes is very effective.

Symptoms

Loud snoring, breathing stops noticed by partner, restless sleep, excessive daytime sleepiness, concentration problems, morning headaches, irritability. Increases the risk of high blood pressure and cardiovascular disease.

Diagnosis

Polysomnography (comprehensive sleep study in the sleep lab): we measure breathing, oxygen, brain activity, heart rhythm and muscle movements during your sleep. Sometimes a screening test at home first.

Treatment

CPAP therapy (mask that keeps your airways open during sleep) is the standard treatment. Additionally: weight reduction, sleeping on your side, avoiding alcohol before bedtime. For milder forms: mandibular advancement device (MAD).

When to see a doctor?

If you experience daily fatigue despite sufficient sleep, loud snoring with breathing stops, or if your partner is concerned about your breathing at night.

🫧 Interstitial lung diseases

In interstitial lung diseases, scar tissue (fibrosis) forms in the lungs, reducing oxygen absorption. You notice this through increasing breathlessness and a dry cough. Early detection and treatment are important to preserve lung function for as long as possible.

Symptoms

Gradually increasing breathlessness (first during exercise, later at rest), dry irritating cough, fatigue. Sometimes crackling sounds heard with the stethoscope ('velcro crackles') and clubbing of the fingers.

Diagnosis

HRCT scan (high-resolution CT) shows the pattern of fibrosis, pulmonary function test with diffusion measurement (DLCO), blood tests for autoimmune diseases, sometimes lung biopsy via thoracoscopy.

Treatment

Antifibrotic medication (pirfenidone or nintedanib) slows the decline in pulmonary fibrosis (IPF). For other forms: immunosuppressive therapy. Oxygen therapy and pulmonary rehabilitation improve quality of life. In exceptional cases: lung transplantation.

When to see a doctor?

If you experience unexplained breathlessness that slowly worsens, dry cough lasting more than 8 weeks, or if you have an autoimmune disease and develop lung symptoms.

🌿 Pleural disorders

The membrane around your lungs (the pleura) can become inflamed or fluid can accumulate. This causes pain when breathing and breathlessness. We diagnose this by ultrasound and can safely drain the fluid if needed.

Symptoms

Sharp, stabbing chest pain that worsens on deep breathing or coughing (pleurisy), breathlessness with fluid accumulation (pleural effusion). Sometimes fever with infection.

Diagnosis

Chest ultrasound (quick and painless), chest X-ray, CT scan. For pleural effusion: puncture (thoracentesis) to analyse the fluid and determine the cause.

Treatment

Treatment of the underlying cause (infection, heart failure, cancer). For large amounts of fluid: drainage via puncture or chest drain. For recurrent fluid: pleurodesis (fusing the pleural layers) or tunnelled drain for home use.

When to see a doctor?

If you experience chest pain when breathing, increasing breathlessness, or fever with chest pain.

🦠 Flu & infections

Respiratory infections caused by viruses or bacteria — from severe influenza to tuberculosis — can seriously affect the lungs. Symptoms include fever, coughing, breathlessness and general malaise. We investigate the cause and start the appropriate treatment.

Symptoms

Fever, cough, sore throat, muscle pain, fatigue, headache. In severe infections: high fever, breathlessness, confusion. At-risk patients: elderly, pregnant women, people with heart or lung disease.

Diagnosis

Clinical examination, possibly nasal swab (PCR test for influenza/COVID/RSV), chest X-ray if pneumonia suspected, blood tests.

Treatment

Rest, fluid intake, fever reduction. For influenza, antiviral medication (oseltamivir) can help if started early. Prevention: annual flu vaccination, COVID vaccination, hand hygiene.

When to see a doctor?

If you have a fever lasting more than 5 days, severe breathlessness, chest pain, or if you belong to a risk group.

Expert clinics within Pulmonology

Besides general consultations, the department offers specialised clinics.

Sleep clinic

Do you sleep poorly, snore loudly or feel extremely tired during the day? In our sleep laboratory, we examine your sleep with sensors that measure your breathing, heart rate and brain activity. Based on this, we create a personalised treatment plan.

Who is it for?

Patients with loud snoring, suspected sleep apnoea, excessive daytime sleepiness, restless sleep, or unexplained fatigue. Also for drivers and pilots who need a medical certificate.

What to expect?

After a consultation with the sleep specialist, you will be invited for a polysomnography (sleep study). You sleep one night in our sleep lab, where sensors record your breathing, brain waves, heart rhythm and leg movements. The examination is painless. Results are usually discussed within 2 weeks.

Team

Dr V. Adam and Dr H. Nguyen (pulmonologists and sleep specialists), sleep technologists, specialised nurses.

Practical

Sleep lab at Aalst — Moorselbaan campus. Appointment via the secretariat. Bring comfortable sleepwear. Avoid caffeine on the day of the examination.

Smoking Cessation Clinic

Quitting smoking is the best investment in your lung health, and you don't have to do it alone. Our certified tobacco specialists guide you step by step with personal advice, medication and motivational counselling. Contact us at rookstopkliniek@azorg.be

Who is it for?

Anyone who wants to quit smoking — whether you have been smoking for 5 or 40 years. Also for e-cigarette users who want to cut down. Special attention for patients with COPD, asthma, lung cancer or cardiovascular disease.

What to expect?

A personal intake interview to discuss your smoking habits and motivation. Then a tailored programme: nicotine replacements, medication, and regular motivational counselling. On average 6 to 8 sessions over 3 months. Reimbursement options through health insurance.

Team

Certified tobacco specialists: specialised nurses and psychologists with additional training in smoking cessation support.

Practical

Available at all campuses. Contact: rookstopkliniek@azorg.be. No referral letter needed.

Sports Clinic & Diving Medicine

Whether you are a top athlete or a recreational exerciser: we test your lung capacity and exercise tolerance. We also provide diving medical examinations. Ideal for those who want to exercise safely with a lung condition.

Who is it for?

Athletes (recreational and competitive) who want to test their lung function and exercise capacity. Patients with lung conditions who want to exercise safely. Divers who need a medical certificate.

What to expect?

Cardiopulmonary exercise test (CPET) on a bicycle: you cycle with increasing resistance while we measure breathing, heart rate, blood pressure and oxygen. For divers: additional lung function examination and specific diving medical assessment. Personalised results and advice.

Team

Dr H. Nguyen (pulmonologist, sports physician, diving medicine specialist).

Practical

Aalst — Moorselbaan campus. Bring sportswear and trainers. Eat lightly 2 hours before the test. Duration: approximately 45 minutes.

Pulmonary rehabilitation

After a serious lung disease, lung cancer treatment or COVID, our rehabilitation programme helps you get back on your feet. A team of pulmonologists, physiotherapists and psychologists work together on your fitness, breathing technique and quality of life.

Who is it for?

Patients with COPD, asthma, pulmonary fibrosis, lung cancer (before/after treatment), post-COVID with persistent symptoms, or other chronic lung diseases leading to reduced fitness and breathlessness.

What to expect?

A multidisciplinary programme of 3 to 6 months: 2 to 3 sessions per week with exercise training, breathing exercises, education about your disease, nutritional advice and psychological support. Before and after the programme, we measure your progress with an exercise test.

Team

Dr An Pletinckx (pulmonologist-rehabilitation specialist), physiotherapists specialised in respiratory rehabilitation, dietitians, psychologists, social workers.

Practical

Aalst — Moorselbaan campus. Referral by pulmonologist or GP. Largely reimbursed by health insurance. Bring sportswear.

Lung Cancer Clinic (MOC)

Every lung cancer patient is discussed weekly in a multidisciplinary oncology meeting (MDT). Pulmonologists, surgeons, oncologists and radiologists jointly decide on the best treatment plan — tailored to your situation.

Who is it for?

All patients with (suspected) lung cancer, lung nodules discovered on CT scan, or mesothelioma. Every patient is personally discussed — even if referred from another hospital.

What to expect?

After diagnosis, we discuss your case at the weekly Multidisciplinary Oncology Meeting (MDT). Pulmonologists, thoracic surgeons, medical oncologists, radiotherapists, radiologists and pathologists participate together. You receive a personalised treatment plan, explained by your pulmonologist. We guide you through the entire process.

Team

Pulmonologists, thoracic surgeons, medical oncologists, radiotherapists, radiologists, pathologists, oncology nurses, psychologists, palliative care team.

Practical

The MDT takes place weekly at Aalst — Moorselbaan campus. Your pulmonologist registers your case. Results are usually discussed with you within a few days.

🧬

Clinical scientific research

Our department actively participates in national and international studies on new treatments. As a patient, you may be eligible for innovative therapies. We are affiliated with Ghent University as a training centre.

Who is it for?

Patients who are eligible for clinical studies on new treatments for asthma, COPD, lung cancer, pulmonary fibrosis or sleep apnoea. Participation is always voluntary and discussed in detail.

What to expect?

Your pulmonologist discusses the possibility of participating in a study. You receive detailed information and sign a consent form. During the study, you are closely monitored with additional consultations and examinations. You can stop at any time.

Team

Study coordinators (nurses), pulmonologist-researchers, data managers. Affiliated with Ghent University as an academic training centre.

Practical

Studies take place at Aalst — Moorselbaan campus. Travel expenses may be reimbursed. Ask your pulmonologist if there is an ongoing study for your condition.
